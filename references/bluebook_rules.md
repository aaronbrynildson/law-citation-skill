# Legal Citation Rule Reference — Footnotes

This reference provides Indigo Book-compatible and Bluebook-style guidance for checking citations in law review and journal footnotes, including space law, national security law, and international law sources. It is an implementation aid for this skill, not a substitute for the current official Bluebook or a journal's house style guide.

---

## R1/R2 — Typeface in Law Review Footnotes

| Source type            | Title / Name           | Author / Parties     |
|------------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Cases                  | *Italic*               | *Italic*             |
| Journal articles       | *Italic*               | Small Caps           |
| Books                  | SMALL CAPS             | SMALL CAPS           |
| Newspaper/magazine     | *Italic*               | Roman                |
| Supra / infra / Id.    | *Italic*               | —                    |
| Treaties               | Roman (not italic)     | —                    |
| Statutes               | Roman                  | —                    |
| Constitutions          | Roman                  | —                    |
| UN documents           | Roman                  | —                    |

**Note:** In law review main text, case names are in ordinary roman type. In footnotes, they are italic.

## R4 — Short Citation Forms

- **Id.** — refers to immediately preceding authority only. Always italicised. Always has a period. Cannot follow an intervening citation.
- **Supra note N** — for non-case, non-statute sources cited earlier. *supra* is italic; "note N" is roman.
- **Infra note N** — for sources cited later. *infra* is italic.
- **[hereinafter X]** — defined in brackets immediately following the first full citation. Used as short form thereafter.
- **Never use supra for cases or statutes.** Use the standard short form: *Brown*, 347 U.S. at 495; § 1983.
- Always write "supra note N" — not "supra N" or "supra fn. N".
- *supra* note number must point backward (not forward, not to itself).

**Case short forms:**
```
Full:  Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954).
Id.:   Id. at 496.                     [immediately following, no intervening cite]
Short: Brown, 347 U.S. at 497.         [after intervening cite]
```

**Statute short forms:**
```
Full:  42 U.S.C. § 1983 (2018).
Short: § 1983.   or   id. § 1984.
```

## R5 — Signals

**Order within a citation sentence:**
1. [no signal] — directly states the proposition
2. *E.g.,*
3. *Accord*
4. *See*
5. *See also*
6. *Cf.*
7. *Compare ... with ...*
8. *Contra*
9. *But see*
10. *But cf.*
11. *See generally*

**Capitalisation:**
- Capitalise at the start of a citation sentence.
- After a semicolon (within the same sentence), lowercase the signal.
- *Cf.* always requires a parenthetical explanation.
- *E.g.,* always has a comma after it.

---

## R6 — Quotations and Parentheticals

- Explanatory parentheticals begin with a lowercase present-participial phrase: *(holding that ...)*, *(noting that ...)*.
- Alterations to quoted text: use brackets [ ], not parentheses ( ).
- Omissions: spaced ellipsis (. . .) — not unspaced (...). Four dots (. . . .) when omission ends a sentence.
- Quotations within parentheticals: lowercase the first letter unless the quote begins a sentence or is a proper noun.

## R10 — Domestic Cases

**Basic format:**
*Plaintiff v. Defendant*, Volume Reporter FirstPage, Pinpoint (Court Year).

- Use "v." not "vs."
- "In re" (not "In Re")
- En-dash (–) in page ranges, not hyphen (-)
- U.S. Supreme Court: cite to U.S. reporter if available; otherwise S. Ct.
- Omit court identifier when obvious from reporter (U.S. = Supreme Court)
- Include circuit number for courts of appeals: (9th Cir. 2020)
- Party is "United States" not "United States of America"
- Omit "Inc.", "Corp.", "Ltd." unless sole identifier

**Examples:**
```
Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954).
United States v. Nixon, 418 U.S. 683, 703 (1974).
Chevron U.S.A., Inc. v. Natural Res. Def. Council, 467 U.S. 837, 842 (1984).
Comm. for Nuclear Responsibility v. Seaborg, 463 F.2d 788, 792 (D.C. Cir. 1971).
```

## R11 — Constitutions

**Format:**
U.S. Const. art. I, § 8, cl. 3.
U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1.

- Abbreviate "Constitution" as "Const."
- Use "art." not "Article", "amend." not "Amendment"
- Sections within constitutional provisions: § (not "sec.")
- Foreign constitutions: [Country] Const. art. [N].

**Examples:**
```
U.S. Const. art. II, § 2, cl. 1.
U.S. Const. amend. I.
U.S. Const. amend. IV.
Fr. Const. art. 15.
```

## R12 — Statutes

**US Code:**
```
42 U.S.C. § 1983 (2018).
51 U.S.C. § 51303 (2018).    [space law: Title 51]
Multiple sections: 42 U.S.C. §§ 1983–1985 (2018).
```

**CFR:**
```
14 C.F.R. § 91.1 (2023).     [FAA regulations]
47 C.F.R. § 25.114 (2023).   [FCC space station rules]
```

**Public Law / Session Law:**
```
Pub. L. No. 111-148, 124 Stat. 119 (2010).
U.S. Commercial Space Launch Competitiveness Act, Pub. L. No. 114-90, § 402, 129 Stat. 704, 720 (2015) (codified at 51 U.S.C. § 51303).
```

**Key rules:**
- Always § or §§ before section numbers; space between symbol and number.
- Year: year of code edition in parentheses at end — not enactment year (unless citing session law).
- Multiple sections: §§ with en-dash.
- Do not cite "2nd" or "3rd" edition — use "2d", "3d".

## R13 — Legislative Materials

**Bills:**
```
H.R. 5515, 115th Cong. (2018).
S. 2700, 117th Cong. (2022).
```

**Committee Reports:**
```
S. Rep. No. 115-262, at 12 (2018).
H.R. Rep. No. 114-92, at 5–6 (2015).
```
- Use "at N" for pinpoint, not "p. N".
- Include Congress number.

**Hearings:**
```
Space Weather: Understanding the Threat and Preparing for the Hazard: Hearing Before the
S. Comm. on Commerce, Sci., & Transp., 115th Cong. 14 (2017) (statement of Thomas Zurbuchen,
NASA Associate Administrator).
```
- Include committee name, Congress number, page, and statement attribution if applicable.

**Congressional Record:**
```
157 Cong. Rec. S1234 (daily ed. Mar. 1, 2011) (statement of Sen. Nelson).
```
- Abbreviated "Cong. Rec." (not "Congressional Record", not "Cong. Record").
- Include volume, page with chamber prefix (S = Senate, H = House), and date.
- For daily edition: add "(daily ed. Mon. Day, Year)".

**Conference Reports:**
```
H.R. Conf. Rep. No. 114-270, at 3 (2015).
```

## R14 — Administrative and Executive Materials

**Executive Orders:**
```
Exec. Order No. 13,772, 3 C.F.R. 52 (2017), reprinted in 3 U.S.C. § 301 note.
Exec. Order No. 13,914, 85 Fed. Reg. 19,119 (Apr. 6, 2020).   [space resources EO]
```
- Format: "Exec. Order No. NNNNN, N C.F.R. N (Year)" or "N Fed. Reg. N (Date)".

**Federal Register:**
```
Streamlining Regulations on Commercial Use of Space, 85 Fed. Reg. 34,935 (June 9, 2020).
```
- "Fed. Reg." not "Federal Register" or "FR".

**Agency Guidance / Advisory Circulars:**
```
FAA, Advisory Circular No. 91-57B, Model Aircraft Operating Standards (2015).
FCC, Report and Order, In the Matter of Streamlining Licensing Procedures for Small Satellites,
  IB Docket No. 18-86, FCC 18-44 (Apr. 19, 2018).
```
- Include issuing agency, document type, title, and date.

**Presidential Proclamations:**
```
Proclamation No. 9,844, 84 Fed. Reg. 4,949 (Feb. 15, 2019).
```

## R15 — Books

**Format:**
AUTHOR LAST NAME, TITLE page (edition year).

```
Manfred Lachs, The Law of Outer Space: An Experience in Contemporary Law-Making 45 (1972).
Frans von der Dunk & Fabio Tronchetti, Handbook of Space Law 112 (2015).
Richard A. Posner, Economic Analysis of Law 45 (9th ed. 2014).
```

**Edited volumes:**
```
Frans von der Dunk, Space Law in the Age of the Nano-Satellite, in Handbook of Space Law 745, 762
  (Frans von der Dunk & Fabio Tronchetti eds., 2015).
```

**Key rules:**
- Author and title in SMALL CAPS.
- Edition ordinals: 2d, 3d, 4th (not 2nd, 3rd).
- Do not use "pp." before page numbers.
- For edited volumes: "(Editor Ed." or "Editors eds., year)".
- Short form: Author, *supra* note N, at page.
- Institutional authors: treat as author in SMALL CAPS.

## R16 — Journal Articles

**Format:**
Author, *Title*, Volume Journal First Page, Pinpoint (Year).

```
Frans von der Dunk, The Origins of Authorisation: Article VI of the Outer Space Treaty,
  National Space Legislation and the Notion of 'Appropriate State', 1 J. Space L. 1, 12 (2011).
Fabio Tronchetti, The Exploitation of Natural Resources of the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies,
  5 Ann. Air & Space L. 323, 335 (2009).
```

**Key rules:**
- Article title in italics; journal name in SMALL CAPS according to the publication's house style.
- Do not use "vol." before volume number.
- Omit issue number ("no. X").
- Year in parentheses at end.
- Journal abbreviated per T13.
- Include pinpoint when citing a specific passage.
- Short form: Author, *supra* note N, at pinpoint.

## R16.6 — Newspapers and Magazines

**Format:**
Author, *Title*, Publication (Mon. Day, Year), URL (last visited Mon. Day, Year).

```
Eric Lipton, *Space Race: The Pentagon Wants to Know What China Is Doing in Orbit*,
  N.Y. Times (Mar. 14, 2023), https://... (last visited Apr. 1, 2026).
```

**Key rules:**
- Article title in italics; publication name in roman.
- Include full date (month abbreviated per T12).
- For online versions, include URL and last-visited date.
- Publication abbreviations: N.Y. Times, Wall St. J., Wash. Post, Fin. Times.

## R17 — Unpublished and Forthcoming Works

**Unpublished manuscript:**
```
Alex Rivera, Stealth Satellites and International Law (unpublished manuscript) (on file with author).
```

**Forthcoming:**
```
Alex Rivera, Space Force JAG Corps, J. Nat'l Sec. L. & Pol'y (forthcoming 2026).
```

**Working paper:**
```
Brian Weeden & Victoria Samson, Global Counterspace Capabilities: An Open Source Assessment
  (Secure World Foundation, Working Paper No. 5, 2023).
```

**Key rules:**
- Unpublished: note "(unpublished manuscript)" and where on file.
- Forthcoming: note "(forthcoming YYYY)".
- Working papers: include series name and number.
- Dissertations: Author, Title (year) (unpublished Ph.D. dissertation, University Name).

## R18 — Internet Sources

**Format:**
Author, *Title*, Source (Mon. Day, Year), URL (last visited Mon. Day, Year).

```
Laura Grego, *A History of Anti-Satellite Programs*, Union of Concerned Scientists (Jan. 2012),
  https://www.ucsusa.org/... (last visited Apr. 1, 2026).
```

**Key rules:**
- Include author if identifiable.
- Article/page title in italics.
- If the source has a print version, cite print and add URL as parallel citation.
- "last visited" date is mandatory; abbreviate month per T12.
- May, June, July are not abbreviated.

## R18 — AI-Generated Output

Current legal citation manuals increasingly treat AI-generated output as a preservation problem, not just a URL problem.

**Minimum review checklist:**
- Identify the tool/model or provider.
- Preserve enough prompt/output detail for another reader to understand what was generated.
- Include durable retrieval evidence where required: saved PDF, screenshot, transcript, archive link, or equivalent.
- Do not cite AI output as legal authority unless the user's publication context permits it.

Flag these for manual review unless the document's journal or court style guide gives a specific accepted form.

## Current Manual / House Style — Tribal Materials

Tribal materials have dedicated current-manual treatment and often require jurisdiction-specific verification.

**Minimum review checklist:**
- Identify the Tribal jurisdiction clearly.
- Identify court/code/source name, section or reporter, date, and online locator if used.
- Check whether the Tribe publishes its own preferred citation form.
- Do not force Tribal sources into ordinary state/federal case or statute formats without verification.

---

## Current Manual / House Style — Archival and Historical Materials

Archival citations should let a reader find the item in a repository.

**Minimum review checklist:**
- Creator/title or item description.
- Date, if known.
- Collection name.
- Repository/library/archive name and location when needed.
- Box, folder, item, reel, or other locator when available.

## R21.4 — Treaties and International Agreements

**Full citation format (multilateral):**
Name of Treaty, art. X, opened for signature Mon. D, YYYY, Vol. U.N.T.S. Page (entered into force Mon. D, YYYY).

**Full citation format (bilateral):**
Name of Agreement, Party 1-Party 2, art. X, Mon. DD, YYYY, Vol. U.N.T.S. Page.

**Key rules:**
- Treaty name in roman type (not italic, not SMALL CAPS).
- Cite to U.N.T.S. first; if not yet in U.N.T.S., cite I.L.M., U.S.T., T.I.A.S., or E.T.S.
- Omit parties for multilateral treaties.
- Use "art." not "Article"; "arts." for multiple articles; "pmbl." for preamble.
- Opening date: "Mon. D, YYYY" format.
- Short form: Treaty Short Name art. IV. or Treaty Short Name, supra note N, art. IV.

**The five major space treaties:**
```
Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space,
  Including the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies, opened for signature Jan. 27, 1967,
  18 U.S.T. 2410, 610 U.N.T.S. 205 (entered into force Oct. 10, 1967).
  [Short form: Outer Space Treaty art. IV.]

Agreement on the Rescue of Astronauts, the Return of Astronauts and the Return of Objects
  Launched into Outer Space, opened for signature Apr. 22, 1968,
  19 U.S.T. 7570, 672 U.N.T.S. 119 (entered into force Dec. 3, 1968).
  [Short form: Rescue Agreement art. 2.]

Convention on International Liability for Damage Caused by Space Objects, opened for signature
  Mar. 29, 1972, 24 U.S.T. 2389, 961 U.N.T.S. 187 (entered into force Sept. 1, 1972).
  [Short form: Liability Convention art. II.]

Convention on Registration of Objects Launched into Outer Space, opened for signature
  Jan. 14, 1975, 28 U.S.T. 695, 1023 U.N.T.S. 15 (entered into force Sept. 15, 1976).
  [Short form: Registration Convention art. II.]

Agreement Governing the Activities of States on the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies,
  opened for signature Dec. 18, 1979, 1363 U.N.T.S. 3 (entered into force July 11, 1984).
  [Short form: Moon Agreement art. 11.]
```

## R21.5/6 — ICJ and International Tribunals

**ICJ format:**
*Case Name* (Party v. Party), Judgment/Advisory Opinion, [Year] I.C.J. Reports FirstPage, Pinpoint (Mon. Day, Year).

**Key rules:**
- Case name in italic.
- Reporter: "I.C.J. Reports" (not "ICJ Rep.", "I.C.J. Rep.", or "ICJ Reports").
- Year of decision in parentheses at end (or bracketed before reporter).
- Use ¶ for paragraph references (not "para." or "¶¶" for multiple).
- Advisory Opinions: include "Advisory Opinion" after case name.
- ITLOS: cite to ITLOS Reports or IICLR.
- WTO: cite to WTO document symbol.

**Examples:**
```
*North Sea Continental Shelf* (F.R.G. v. Den.; F.R.G. v. Neth.), Judgment, 1969 I.C.J. Reports 3, 46 (Feb. 20).
*Legality of the Threat or Use of Nuclear Weapons*, Advisory Opinion, 1996 I.C.J. Reports 226, 240 (July 8).
*Corfu Channel* (U.K. v. Alb.), Judgment, 1949 I.C.J. Reports 4, 22 (Apr. 9).
```

## R21.8 — United Nations Documents

**General Assembly Resolutions:**
```
G.A. Res. 1348 (XIII) (Dec. 13, 1958).
G.A. Res. 1884 (XVIII) (Oct. 17, 1963).   [space arms control resolution]
G.A. Res. 47/68 (Dec. 14, 1992).          [nuclear power sources principles]
```

**Security Council Resolutions:**
```
S.C. Res. 827, U.N. Doc. S/RES/827 (May 25, 1993).
```

**UN Documents:**
```
U.N. Comm. on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space, Rep. of the Legal Subcomm.,
  U.N. Doc. A/AC.105/1122 (2021).
U.N. Doc. A/AC.105/C.2/SR.1 (date).
```

**GAOR:**
```
U.N. GAOR, 12th Sess., Supp. No. 16, at 5, U.N. Doc. A/3689 (1957).
```

**Key rules:**
- "G.A. Res." not "UNGA Res." or "GA Res."
- "S.C. Res." not "UNSC Res." or "SC Res."
- "U.N. GAOR" not "UNGAOR" or "UN GAOR"
- "U.N. Doc." not "UN Doc." or "U.N. Document"
- Always include the year in parentheses.
- GAOR: include session, supplement number, page, and document symbol.
